About the Teacher

Born in Korea and raised in Canada, Ja Young (Alice) Jeon pursued her higher musical education in Germany, where she studied violin performance in Mainz, Darmstadt, and Freiburg, earning both her Bachelor's and Master's degrees. During her time in Freiburg, she also taught young string students through elementary school music programs, developing a deep passion for music education alongside her performing career.

Throughout her studies and professional career, she has performed extensively as a soloist, orchestral musician, chamber artist, festival participant, and competition prizewinner across Germany and Canada. Alongside her performing career, she developed a strong passion for teaching, working with private students as well as young string players in elementary school music programs.

Teaching Philosophy

Alice believes that learning the violin is about much more than mastering an instrument. Music education provides an opportunity to develop confidence, discipline, creativity, perseverance, and meaningful self-expression.

Her teaching combines strong technical foundations with thoughtful musical development, tailoring each lesson to the individual needs and goals of every student. Whether a student is learning for enjoyment, preparing for RCM examinations, or pursuing advanced musical studies, Alice strives to create a supportive and inspiring environment where students can grow both as musicians and as individuals.

Through music, she hopes to nurture not only skilled violinists, but also curious, expressive, and lifelong lovers of the arts.

After many years abroad, Alice returned to Vancouver, where she is dedicated to mentoring the next generation of musicians and sharing the joy and beauty of music with her community.
